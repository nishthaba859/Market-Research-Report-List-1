<p><ul><li>Isocentric C-arms</li><li>Non Isocentric C-arms</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Surgical 3D Mobile C-Arm market is primarily categorized into two types: isocentric and non-isocentric C-arms. Isocentric C-arms maintain a fixed focal point while allowing for versatile movement and rotation, optimizing imaging quality and facilitating complex surgical procedures. Non-isocentric C-arms, in contrast, have pivot points that vary with movement, making them more suitable for straightforward applications. Both types serve essential roles in various medical fields, particularly in orthopedic, neurosurgery, and dental surgeries, by enhancing real-time imaging capabilities.</p></p>
